2050 Game

This is a prototype for a board game that is intended to communicate how future changes in energy infrastructure will affect the country. The goal of the game is to match the energy capacity suggested in the "Energy Strategy 2050" by the Danish government.

The gameplay loop somewhat resembles classic board games like Monopoly. The overall objective is to place enough turbines to cover the required energy capacity. Several challenges are presented though which must be worked around.

The player is assigned a budget that must be complied with. Wind turbines can be placed on the game board on a successful dice roll. If the dice roll is failed, the wind turbines cannot be build, and a "consequence" card will be drawn that applies negative effects such as angry neighbours or political initiatives that will limit the construction process. The player can decide between offshore wind turbines that are easier to get permission to build, but also more expensive, or onshore which is cheaper but pose other challenges.

The game seeks to invoke reflection in the player, both in terms of what must change in order to meet the energy goals, and also the challenges that will occur if the entire energy capacity must be achieved through wind energy.
