This'n'That

This'n'That is a toy targeted towards adults that strives to relieve stress and enhance connectivity. It consists of 2 soft spheres that can be squeezed and moulded into different shapes. When one unit is manipulated, feedback is given on the other unit.

The prototype consists of 2 units. Both contain Arduino microcontrollers that are connected wirelessly on a private network that is similar to Bluetooth but uses another protocol. The prototypes were then fitted with sensors, motors, buzzers, and light sources, which were tested to determine which kinds of feedback would be most desirable for the final product.

The prototypes communicate in a manner, where one toy takes measurements of all its inputs, then format them into an organised sequence of binary data which then is loaded into a packet and sent into the other. When the other toy receives this packet, it distributes the data into an array that can be converted into a reaction locally. The second toy then also goes through the same process of taking measurements from inputs and sending them, leading to a continuous back and forth between the two parts for as long as they stay connected.
