This is a Twitter bot that attempts to channel activism through social media in a playful way.

The purpose of this Twitter bot is to target a few selected hashtags that are connected to food, and transform the experiences concerning these hashtags into something completely contradictory by replying with photos of starving African children. A lot of the interaction on social media is shallow and the purpose of this project was to make people aware of problems in other parts of the world, at least on subconscious level. The reactions to the bot were mixed. Of the ~1200 replies made by the bot, most were ignored. When the bot got a reply from the recipient, some of them were negative although most people absorbed the message and were positive. The bot even gained 6 followers. All in all it was an experiment, and it was concluded that there is potential for conveying a serious message on social media.

The bot was written in Python using the REST API. In plain language the bot conducts the following actions:

  1. 1   Search for tweet containing specific hashtag. (#foodporrn, #foodporn, #instafood, #yummy, #foodie, or #foodgasm)

  2. 2   If the tweet is not sent by a user account already targeted by a HungerGram tweet, proceed.

  3. 3   Check if the tweet contains a photo or a link to an image file on Instagram. If it does, continue to compose a tweet and send it at the analysed tweet.

  4. 4   Choose random photo and text string from preset list and compose tweet for reply to the analysed tweet.

  5. 5   The ID of the user account posting the original tweet is added to a list, in order to avoid tweeting the same account again.

  6. 6  A break of 30 to 70 seconds is taken to emulate writing and posting time of a tweet. This is done to avoid the bot being flagged as an automated process.

  7. 7  Rinse and repeat.
